# Microsoft — Numbers, units, dates, time

Source: https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/style-guide/numbers, https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/style-guide/a-z-word-list-term-collections/term-collections/date-time-terms
Last refreshed: 2026-04-29

## Numerals vs spelled-out

- In body text, spell out zero through nine; use numerals for 10 and above.
- Always use numerals for measurements (distance, weight, temperature, pixels, points) even when the value is below 10: "3 feet," "0.75 grams."
- Always use numerals for: time of day, percentages, table/worksheet coordinates, numbered document sections.
- If one item in a list requires a numeral, use numerals for all items of that type: "one has 16 pages, one has 7 pages, one has 5 pages."
- When two numbers referring to different things appear together, spell out one and use a numeral for the other: "fifteen 20-page articles."
- Do not start a sentence with a numeral. Add a modifier or rephrase: "More than 10 apps…" or spell it out: "Eleven apps…"
- It is acceptable to start list items with numerals.

## Units of measurement

- For measurements, use numerals even below 10: "3 cm," "5 inches."
- Spell out units in running text unless space is constrained (tables, UI).
- Dimensions: spell out "by" in prose; use the multiplication sign (×) with spaces before and after for tile sizes, screen resolutions, and paper sizes: "4 × 4 tile," "1280 × 1024," "8.5" × 11" paper."
- Do not abbreviate "thousand," "million," or "billion" as K, M, or B in body text. Write out "65,000" or "65 thousand." Reserve abbreviations for severely space-constrained UI only; if used, capitalize (K, M, B), no space between numeral and abbreviation.
- For units of time in space-constrained contexts: h (hour), min (minute), sec (second), ms (millisecond). Spell out nanosecond and microsecond — no abbreviation.

## Dates

- Format: month day, year — "July 31, 2016." Do not use day-month-year order in English content.
- Always spell out the month name to avoid regional ambiguity. Do not use numeric-only formats like "6/12/2017" in prose (acceptable in UI when locale-aware code handles formatting).
- Do not use ordinal numbers in dates: "June 1," not "June 1st" or "June first."
- Three-letter month abbreviations (Jan, Feb, Mar, Apr, May, Jun, Jul, Aug, Sep, Oct, Nov, Dec) are acceptable when space is severely limited. No trailing period. Capitalize first letter only.
- Capitalize days of the week. Do not abbreviate unless space is extremely limited; preferred three-letter abbreviations: Sun, Mon, Tue, Wed, Thu, Fri, Sat.
- Do not reference seasons; use months or quarters instead.

## Time

- Use numerals for all times of day: "10:45 AM," "6:30 PM."
- Always include AM or PM (uppercase, preceded by one space).
- Use "noon" and "midnight" instead of "12:00 PM" / "12:00 AM."
- Include the time zone when discussing events that span time zones.
- Time ranges in text: use "to" — "10:00 AM to 2:00 PM."
- Time zones: capitalize; spell out unless space is severely limited. Preferred form: "Eastern Time (UTC-5)." Acceptable abbreviations: ET, CT, MT, PT. Do not specify "standard" vs "daylight saving" unless the distinction matters for a specific event. Use "Coordinated Universal Time (UTC)," not "GMT," not "Universal Time Coordinated."

## Currency

- Use a comma for thousands, period for the decimal point.
- Place the currency symbol immediately before the numeral, no space: "$1,024."
- Use commas for four or more digits: "$1,024," not "$1024."
- Spell out the currency name when the symbol may be ambiguous in a global context.

## Ranges and percentages

- Ranges in body text: use "from … through" — "from 9 through 17."
- Time ranges in text: use "to" — "10:00 AM to 2:00 PM."
- Do not use en dashes for ranges in prose. En dashes are acceptable in tables, UI, and page ranges where space is constrained.
- Percentages: always use numerals, no matter how small. Use "percent" (spelled out) as the unit — "50 percent," "1 percent." Use "percentage" when no specific quantity is given.

## Phone numbers, fractions, ordinals

**Phone numbers:** Use hyphens only to separate the parts of a US/Canadian number: "612-555-0175." Do not use parentheses, periods, or spaces. For other regions, consult the relevant localization guide.

**Fractions:** Express as decimals, words, or symbols — whichever suits the context. Add a zero before the decimal point for values less than one: "0.5 cm." Do not use numerals separated by a slash in prose. Hyphenate spelled-out fractions: "one-third," "two-thirds."

**Ordinals:** Always spell out ordinal numbers: "the first row," "the twenty-first anniversary." Do not add "-ly" to ordinals (not "firstly"). Do not use ordinal numbers for dates.

**Negative numbers:** Use a minus sign, not a hyphen or en dash: "−79."

**Compound numbers:** Hyphenate when spelled out: "twenty-five fonts," "the twenty-first day."
