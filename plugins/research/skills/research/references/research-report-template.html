Findings

Each finding is an <h3> followed by one or two short paragraphs of evidence. Citations appear as superscript footnote links that scroll to the matching entry under Sources.

1. Token-bucket rate limiting is the dominant pattern in the surveyed APIs

Four of the five surveyed services implement per-key token buckets backed by a shared cache, with refill computed from elapsed time rather than a fixed window[1]. The remaining service uses a sliding-window counter, which trades simplicity for slightly higher memory use[2].

2. Burst capacity is configured separately from sustained rate in every case

All five services expose rate and burst as independent knobs, allowing idle callers to fire short bursts without blowing the long-run budget[1][3]. Defaults vary by an order of magnitude (10 req/min to 600 req/min), so cross-service comparisons require normalising on a single tier[4].

3. Client-side guidance converges on Retry-After plus exponential backoff

Every documented client library inspects the Retry-After header on a 429 response and falls back to capped exponential backoff when the header is absent[3][5]. Two of the libraries additionally jitter the delay to avoid thundering-herd effects after a shared outage[5].
