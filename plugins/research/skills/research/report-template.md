# Report Templates

## Deep Mode Report

Use this structure for deep mode reports:

# {Title}

*Date: {YYYY-MM-DD}*

## Executive Summary
State the thesis in the first paragraph. Then 2-3 paragraphs: key findings that support it, implications, recommendations. The reader should know your argument after reading only this section.

## Table of Contents

## Introduction
Context, importance, scope. End with a clear statement of the report's thesis.

## Methodology
Research approach, perspectives consulted, source types. Flag credibility tiers: independent research vs. consulting firms vs. vendor content. Note limitations of the research itself.

## What Matters Most
The 3-7 most important findings, ranked by significance. This is the core of the report. Each finding should be argued, not just stated — compare sources, note contradictions, take positions. Structure around your thesis.

## Supporting Evidence
### {Subtopic per sub-question/perspective}
Detailed findings with inline citations. Flag source credibility where relevant. Note where sources contradict each other and which you find more credible and why.

## Analysis & Insights
This section must be the strongest part of the report. Synthesize across findings — what patterns emerge? What surprised you? Where does the conventional wisdom get it wrong? What would you tell a decision-maker who has 5 minutes?

## Limitations & Open Problems
What this report's proposed solutions genuinely cannot solve. Be specific: attribution problems, baseline gaps, political barriers, scenarios where the frameworks break down. Address the most common real-world starting point (no baselines, no measurement culture) honestly.

## Future Outlook
Specific, falsifiable predictions with timelines. "Spending will increase" is not a prediction. "By [date], [specific measurable thing] will happen because [reason]" is. If you cannot make falsifiable predictions, cut this section.

## Conclusions & Practical Starting Point
Summary of the thesis and main supporting arguments. Actionable recommendations prioritized by impact.

End with a "Starting from zero" subsection: concrete guidance for the most common real-world scenario — an organization with a few pilots, no baselines, no measurement culture, and leadership asking "is this working?" This is not the ideal path; it is the realistic one.

## References
All sources with URLs and access dates

## Quick Mode Report

Use this structure for quick mode reports:

# {Title}

*Date: {YYYY-MM-DD}*

## Executive Summary
2-3 paragraphs: key findings and takeaways

## Key Findings
### {Subtopic}
Findings with inline citations

## References
All sources with URLs

---

## Citation Style

- Inline citations with links: "According to [Source Name](URL), ..."
- Include publication dates when available
- Format: `[Author/Organization, Year](URL)` or `[Article Title - Publisher](URL)`
- Every non-obvious claim must have a citation
- Key claims cross-referenced (2+ independent sources)
